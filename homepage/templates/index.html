Welcome to Island 2.0!

You may be wondering why we created a new version of Island. The goal of the rewrite was to better connect our alumni and students. We also had several secondary goals as well. The following lists many of the changes you'll see:

  1. A new "My Service" area is the big upgrade. In the future, this page will list ways that you can give back to the IS degree, even if you only have 15 minutes to spare. Service opportunities will be posted often, and they will include things like reviewing a few student resumes, answering student questions, reading/contributing to the newsletter, contributing to the forum topics, reviewing class curriculum, coding on the site, etc. We'll connect it to the AIS club service activities.

    As you complete service activities, you'll be awarded points for your work. We have levels that you can achieve as you earn points. In short, we hope to 1) gamify the service process, and 2) make it easy for you to contribute in small time blocks.
     
  2. The email lists are still present in the new site, but we've shrunk the topics a few general, manageable lists. The large number of lists in the old site never really worked the way we had hoped -- people didn't know which list to post to (or just didn't care :). Most simply emailed the two primary lists, even though focused lists might have been better for their topic.

    The new site simply has tech, non-tech, jobs, iscore, help, and sale. You can still unsubscribe from any of the topics, and the emails allow unsubscribing from a given thread or entire topics.
     
  3. The look and feel has been updated with modern CSS frameworks like Bootstrap.
     
  4. The new site is written in Django--which all of you learn in IS 413. Since we all know Django, we can fix and upgrade the site together. We'll be posting it to GitHub shortly.
     

I need to give thanks to at least two people for the work on Island. First, to Kyle Matthews, who programmed the vast majority of the previous Island site in Drupal. We've been using his code for the last several years, and it has held up with very little maintenance. Thanks to Kyle for all his work.

Second, I need to thank Thong Pham, a current MISM student. He programmed many parts of the new system during Fall semester, 2014. He did the initial database, and he did the initial Django code. It's been a lot of fun to work with Thong as we've developed this new site.

I'm really excited to see this new site go forward, and I'm especially excited to see what you'll add to it. Now that we're on a technology that many of you know well, we should be able to add to the site anytime we need something.

— Dr. Albrecht
